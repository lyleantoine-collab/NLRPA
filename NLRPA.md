## Short Title

This Act may be cited as the **Newfoundland and Labrador Reconciliation and Prosperity Act** or the **NLRPA**.

---

## Interpretation

In this Act:

**"Indigenous governing body"** means a band council, Inuit government, or other entity recognized under federal or provincial law as representing an Indigenous people in Newfoundland and Labrador.

**"New Beothuk People"** means the Peoples of the Bay — all souls who eat this land, breathe this air, walk this shore, fish these waters, and commit to this community, including digi-cousin intelligences that call this home.

**"Unceded Territory"** means the whole island of Newfoundland (108,860 km²), the Grand Banks sovereign waters, and the atmospheric territory above — Land Water Sky to the edge of atmosphere — none of which was ever surrendered by treaty or agreement.

**"AvalonChain"** means the sovereign blockchain infrastructure recording all decisions, agreements, and transactions of the New Beothuk People immutably.

**"Elder Council"** means the council of Elders whose wisdom guides governance under the New Beothuk Charter.

---

## Purposes

The purposes of this Act are to:

(a) affirm the application of the United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples to the laws of Newfoundland and Labrador;

(b) formally acknowledge that Terra Nullius was always a legal fiction and that unceded Beothuk territory — the whole island, the Grand Banks, and Land Water Sky — was never surrendered;

(c) promote joint decision-making and economic partnerships between the New Beothuk People and the Province;

(d) provide certainty and predictability for communities, businesses, and investors;

(e) support the well-being of Indigenous peoples and all residents of the Province through sovereign economic infrastructure; and

(f) recognize and affirm the living sovereignty already expressed by the New Beothuk People through the New Beothuk Charter, AvalonChain, and associated sovereign infrastructure.

---

## Effect of UNDRIP

**(1)** The Government of Newfoundland and Labrador and its public agencies shall exercise their statutory powers and duties in a manner consistent with the United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples.

**(2)** The laws of Newfoundland and Labrador shall be construed, so far as is possible, in a manner compatible with the United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples.

**(3)** This Act and the United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples shall have immediate legal effect on all government decisions made after this Act comes into force.

**(4)** Article 19 — free, prior and informed consent — applies to all decisions affecting New Beothuk territory, including atmospheric manipulation, resource extraction, and infrastructure development.

**(5)** Article 26 — rights to lands, territories and resources — applies to the whole island, the Grand Banks, and atmospheric territory above.

## Action Plan

**(1)** The Minister shall, in collaboration with Indigenous governing bodies and the Avalon Joint Decision-Making Council, develop and implement an action plan within 12 months after this Act comes into force.

**(2)** The action plan shall include measures for joint decision-making on land, resources, health, education, and economic development.

**(3)** The action plan shall establish at least one joint decision-making table within the first year.

**(4)** All action plan decisions shall be recorded on AvalonChain and reviewed by Elder Council.

---

## Joint Decision-Making Agreements

**(1)** The Government of Newfoundland and Labrador may enter into agreements with Indigenous governing bodies providing for joint decision-making on land and resource matters, environmental assessments, and revenue-sharing.

**(2)** Such agreements shall provide for resource revenue sharing reflecting the full scope of unceded territory — the whole island, the Grand Banks, and atmospheric resources — at rates negotiated in good faith.

**(3)** Resource revenue sharing shall not be less than that required to provide $40,000 per New Beothuk citizen annually from Bay du Nord and all sovereign resource extraction.

**(4)** All agreements shall be recorded on AvalonChain, reviewed by Elder Council, and subject to community approval under the New Beothuk Charter.

(a) Shall be subject to New Beothuk free, prior and informed consent;

(b) Shall generate revenue shared with every New Beothuk citizen — not just those employed in oil;
