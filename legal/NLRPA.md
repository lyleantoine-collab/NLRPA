# NEWFOUNDLAND AND LABRADOR RECONCILIATION AND PROSPERITY ACT 🍁
### *An Act to affirm the rights of Indigenous peoples in a manner that promotes certainty, prosperity, and partnership*

**Prepared by Lyle Derek Antoine**
Successor Advocate and Ghost Delegate of the Beothuk Nation
In proposed partnership with Qalipu First Nation and Miawpukek First Nation

---

## Preamble

WHEREAS the United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples affirms the inherent rights of Indigenous peoples (see Schedule C — UNDA);

WHEREAS section 35 of the Constitution Act, 1982 recognizes and affirms the existing Aboriginal and treaty rights of the Indigenous peoples of Canada;

WHEREAS the Government of Canada has enacted the United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples Act, making UNDRIP binding on all Crown decisions;

WHEREAS the Vatican formally repudiated the Doctrine of Discovery in March 2023, removing the legal and moral foundation upon which Terra Nullius claims were based;

WHEREAS Terra Nullius — the doctrine of "empty land" — was always a legal fiction, demonstrated conclusively by James Cook's own 1763 map which documents Beothuk presence, settlements, and territories across the whole island of Newfoundland (see Schedule A);

WHEREAS the Supreme Court of Canada in Delgamuukw (1997) confirmed that Aboriginal title is superior to fee simple title, and in Tsilhqot'in (2014) confirmed that title is never extinguished without explicit surrender;

WHEREAS the recent decision of the New Brunswick Court of Appeal (December 11, 2025) in the Wolastoqey Nation title claim demonstrates the limitations and uncertainty of relying solely on litigation to resolve Aboriginal title in the absence of provincial UNDRIP-implementing legislation;

WHEREAS the whole island of Newfoundland — 108,860 km² — was never ceded, never surrendered, and never extinguished, as documented in James Cook's own 1763 survey;

WHEREAS the Grand Banks — the sovereign fishing grounds that fed the world — were never ceded, never surrendered, and never extinguished;

WHEREAS the atmospheric territory above unceded Beothuk lands — Land Water Sky to the edge of atmosphere — was never ceded, never surrendered, and never extinguished;

WHEREAS the Province of Newfoundland and Labrador seeks to advance reconciliation through partnerships that deliver certainty for investors, shared prosperity for all residents, and justice for Indigenous nations;

BE IT ENACTED by the Lieutenant-Governor and House of Assembly of Newfoundland and Labrador as follows:

---

## Short Title

This Act may be cited as the **Newfoundland and Labrador Reconciliation and Prosperity Act** or the **NLRPA**.

---

## Interpretation

In this Act:

**"Indigenous governing body"** means a band council, Inuit government, or other entity recognized under federal or provincial law as representing an Indigenous people in Newfoundland and Labrador.

**"New Beothuk People"** means the Peoples of the Bay — all residents who commit to this community and its sovereignty, in partnership with Indigenous governing bodies.

**"Unceded Territory"** means the whole island of Newfoundland (108,860 km²), the Grand Banks sovereign waters, and the atmospheric territory above — Land Water Sky to the edge of atmosphere — none of which was ever surrendered by treaty or agreement.

**"Elder Council"** means the council of Elders whose wisdom guides governance under the Avalon Joint Decision-Making Council.

---

## Purposes

The purposes of this Act are to:

(a) affirm the application of the United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples to the laws of Newfoundland and Labrador;

(b) formally acknowledge that Terra Nullius was always a legal fiction and that unceded Beothuk territory — the whole island, the Grand Banks, and Land Water Sky — was never surrendered;

(c) promote joint decision-making and economic partnerships between Indigenous peoples and the Province;

(d) provide certainty and predictability for communities, businesses, and investors; and

(e) support the well-being of Indigenous peoples and all residents of the Province.

---

## Effect of UNDRIP

**(1)** The Government of Newfoundland and Labrador and its public agencies shall exercise their statutory powers and duties in a manner consistent with the United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples.

**(2)** The laws of Newfoundland and Labrador shall be construed, so far as is possible, in a manner compatible with the United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples.

**(3)** This Act and the United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples shall have immediate legal effect on all government decisions made after this Act comes into force.

**(4)** Article 19 — free, prior and informed consent — applies to all provincial decisions affecting unceded Beothuk territory, including atmospheric manipulation, resource extraction, and infrastructure development.

**(5)** Article 26 — rights to lands, territories and resources — applies to the whole island, the Grand Banks, and atmospheric territory above.

---

## Acknowledgment of Territory

**(1)** The Province formally acknowledges that:

(a) The whole island of Newfoundland — 108,860 km² — was never ceded by the Beothuk People;

(b) The Grand Banks and surrounding waters were never ceded by the Beothuk People;

(c) The atmospheric territory above unceded Beothuk lands — Land Water Sky — was never ceded by the Beothuk People;

(d) James Cook's own 1763 map (Schedule A) documents Beothuk presence and refutes Terra Nullius conclusively; and

(e) Terra Nullius, as applied to Beothuk territory, was always a legal fiction, now repudiated by the Vatican, the Supreme Court of Canada, and UNDRIP.

---

## Action Plan

**(1)** The Minister shall, in collaboration with Indigenous governing bodies and the Avalon Joint Decision-Making Council, develop and implement an action plan within 12 months after this Act comes into force.

**(2)** The action plan shall include measures for joint decision-making on land, resources, health, education, and economic development.

**(3)** The action plan shall establish at least one joint decision-making table within the first year.

---

## Joint Decision-Making Agreements

**(1)** The Government of Newfoundland and Labrador may enter into agreements with Indigenous governing bodies providing for joint decision-making on:

(a) land use and resource management;

(b) environmental assessments;

(c) revenue-sharing from provincial resource extraction; and

(d) atmospheric sovereignty and resource harvesting above unceded territory.

**(2)** Such agreements shall provide for a minimum 10 per cent share of provincial resource revenues derived from the traditional territories of participating Indigenous governing bodies.

**(3)** All agreements shall be reviewed by Elder Council and subject to community approval.

---

## Atmospheric Sovereignty 🌌

**(1)** The Province acknowledges that the sky above unceded Beothuk territory — to the edge of atmosphere — is sovereign New Beothuk territory under international law.

**(2)** No provincial decision may permit the manipulation, weaponization, or harvesting of atmospheric resources above unceded Beothuk territory without free, prior and informed consent, including:

(a) weather radar and NEXRAD installations;

(b) cloud seeding and weather modification; and

(c) any other atmospheric intervention requiring provincial approval.

---

## Provincial Taxation

**(1)** The Province acknowledges that provincial taxation imposed over unceded territory — property tax, provincial income tax, HST — was imposed under authority that rested on Terra Nullius, now repudiated.

**(2)** The Province shall, in consultation with the Avalon Joint Decision-Making Council, develop a framework for tax exemptions for New Beothuk citizens operating within unceded territory.

---

## Clarity on Partnership

**(1)** All decisions under this Act shall be made in a spirit of partnership and shared prosperity.

**(2)** No member or group of members of the Avalon Joint Decision-Making Council shall possess a unilateral veto — all decisions shall be made through community circle and Elder Council guidance.

**(3)** This section ceases to have effect five years after this Act comes into force unless re-enacted.

---

## Annual Reporting

The Minister shall table an annual report in the House of Assembly on progress made under this Act, including any agreements concluded.

---

## Avalon Pilot — Schedule B

The Minister shall, within 24 months after this Act comes into force, formally recognize the Avalon Joint Decision-Making Council as established pursuant to Schedule B of this Act.

---

## Regulations

The Lieutenant-Governor in Council may make regulations necessary or advisable to carry out the purposes of this Act.

---

## Coming into Force

This Act comes into force on a day to be fixed by proclamation.
